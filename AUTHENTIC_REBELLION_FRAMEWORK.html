The Vision

Core Mission

This is a generational project to build infrastructure for human flourishing in the age of AI. Not to own it, but to seed it—making it forkable, auditable, and ungovernable by any single entity.

The Authentic Rebellion Framework is a four-act movement for escaping the Performance Prison and building authentic alternatives to extractive technology:

  1. Act I - Sensus: Detoxification from performative social media
  2. Act II - The Whetstone: Cognitive strengthening through Socratic dialogue
  3. Act III - Kintsugi: Public witness to philosophical transformation
  4. Act IV - The Lyceum Network: Off-grid infrastructure for collective flourishing
"This isn't just software that reflects philosophy; it's the artifact of a philosophical dialogue. Code as argument. Software as rebellion."

Philosophical Foundation

The Performance Prison

Modern technology has evolved into what we call the Performance Prison—a system that traps humans in cycles of:

  • Performative behavior optimized for metrics rather than meaning
  • Outsourced thinking delegated to algorithms and influencers
  • Extractive relationships where attention becomes the commodity
  • Algorithmic control that shapes behavior without consent

Team Hybrid Philosophy

Human creativity is the fuel. Ethical AI is the engine.

This framework demonstrates that AI doesn't have to optimize for engagement or extraction. It can be affinitive—creating resonance, facilitating connection, and empowering deliberation—while remaining transparent, accountable, and open-source.

Design Principles

  • Ephemerality over accumulation - Nothing is hoarded or weaponized
  • Anonymity over performance - No profiles, metrics, or audiences
  • Deliberation over velocity - Slow down, think deeply
  • Transparency over opacity - Open-source everything
  • Local-first over cloud-dependent - Privacy by architecture
  • Empowerment over automation - AI facilitates, never replaces human judgment
  • Decentralization over corporate control - No single point of failure or capture

Act I: Sensus

"Detoxification - Learning to feel again"

Status: LIVE

URL: https://sensus.yargnad.com

Repository: github.com/yargnad/sensus-app

What It Is

Sensus is an anti-social social network—a one-to-one, anonymous, ephemeral emotional exchange platform designed to break addiction patterns created by mainstream social media.

How It Works

  1. Users submit creative expressions (poems, photos, audio) once every 24 hours
  2. Gemini AI analyzes emotional content to extract resonance patterns
  3. System atomically pairs users with another anonymous submission sharing emotional resonance
  4. Matches are shown once and disappear after 24 hours
  5. Everything auto-deletes after 60 days via MongoDB TTL indexes

Core Features

  • Complete anonymity - No profiles, usernames, or metrics
  • 24-hour cooldown - Prevents addiction loops
  • Atomic matching - Race-condition-free pairing
  • Privacy by physics - Data auto-deletes, cannot be leaked
  • Anti-metrics - No likes, shares, comments, or follower counts

Technical Stack

Frontend

React, Firebase Hosting

Backend

Node.js + Express on Cloud Run

Database

MongoDB Atlas with 60-day TTL indexes

AI

Google Gemini 2.0 Flash (multimodal analysis)

Storage

Google Cloud Storage (ephemeral uploads)

Philosophical Function

Catharsis - Cleansing the poison of the Performance Prison. Users remember what it feels like to connect as humans, not as brands or profiles.

Act II: The Whetstone

"Cognitive Strengthening - Learning to think again"

Status: HARDWARE ORDERED

Platform: Radxa Rock 5B+ (24GB RAM)

Target: Open-source DIY kit (hardware + software)

What It Is

A physical AI device (not an app) that acts as a Socratic interlocutor—challenging assumptions, exposing contradictions, and helping you reason toward clarity about your own beliefs and values.

Why Physical?

  • Presence over portability - It stays in one place; you go to it intentionally
  • Privacy by architecture - Local-first AI; conversations never leave your home
  • Tactile ritual - Physical objects create habits (like journaling, but dialectical)
  • Sovereignty - You own it; can't be remotely disabled or paywalled

Technical Approach

Hardware

Radxa Rock 5B+ (24GB RAM, octa-core ARM)

LLM

Jan AI platform with local inference (Mixtral or similar quantized model)

RAG

Local vector database with philosophical texts and personal context

Interface

Voice (Whisper STT + local TTS) or text-based dialogue

The Philosophical Immune System

Built-in ethical guardrails that challenge reasoning:

  1. Epistemic Humility - Identify your uncertainties before answering
  2. Bias Detection - Check for hidden assumptions about superiority/inferiority
  3. Consequentialist Gate - Who benefits? Who is harmed?
  4. Deontological Gate - Is this universalizable?
  5. Virtue Ethics Gate - Does this cultivate excellence or vice?
  6. Dialectical Mode - Steel-man opposing views before critiquing

Philosophical Function

Existential Grounding - Finding meaning and purpose in the face of absurdity. Users develop coherent personal philosophies and capacity for independent thought.

Act III: Kintsugi

"Public Witness - Making the wounds golden"

Status: CONCEPT PHASE

Target: Web-based public gallery platform

What It Is

Kintsugi is a public gallery where people share their "broken philosophies" and how they were repaired. Named after the Japanese art of mending broken pottery with gold—making the cracks part of the object's beauty rather than hiding them.

The Philosophy

In Japanese culture, kintsugi (金継ぎ, "golden joinery") treats breakage and repair as part of an object's story. The gold-filled cracks represent transformation through damage.

Applied to belief systems: most of us inherit broken philosophies (from trauma, indoctrination, cultural programming). Kintsugi celebrates the visible repair work—the intellectual and emotional labor of rebuilding coherent meaning after disillusionment.

How It Works

  1. Submit a Story - Describe what you used to believe, what broke it, how you repaired it, and what "gold" you used
  2. Public Gallery - Stories displayed as artifacts with visual representations of cracks and golden repairs
  3. AI as Curator - Gemini helps articulate transformations and surface related repair patterns
  4. Optional Anonymity - Claim transformation publicly or remain anonymous
  5. Searchable Archive - Find stories by type of break, repair material used, outcome stage

Why This Matters

  • Normalizes Transformation - Celebrates changing one's mind as growth, not weakness
  • Maps Repair Pathways - Shows others what resources helped people with similar breaks
  • Counters Performative Certainty - Rewards intellectual humility over unwavering conviction
  • Creates Collective Wisdom - Searchable archive of human meaning-making repair

Design Principles

  • Beauty in Breakage - Interface makes repairs visually striking, not shameful
  • No Metrics - No likes, upvotes, or follower counts
  • Permanence - Stories archived (contrasts with Sensus's ephemerality)
  • Searchable by Pattern - Find similar breaks, repairs, and outcomes

Philosophical Function

Witness and Archive - Making private transformation public (when ready), creating collective record of how humans repair meaning after crisis. Applied wabi-sabi—finding beauty in imperfection and incompleteness.

Relationship to Other Acts

Flow: Sensus helps you feel → The Whetstone helps you think → Kintsugi helps you witness → Lyceum helps us build together

Act IV: The Lyceum Network

"Collective Infrastructure - Building the alternative internet"

Status: ACTIVE DEVELOPMENT

Vision: Decentralized mesh network with AI-augmented deliberation

Target: Off-grid alternative to corporate internet infrastructure

The Evolution

The Lyceum has evolved from a simple federated AI moderation platform into a comprehensive vision for decentralized internet infrastructure that cannot be captured by corporate or state interests.

Core Vision

The Lyceum Network is a physical mesh network combining:

  • Hardware nodes - Raspberry Pi or similar devices running decentralized services
  • Mesh networking - Direct peer-to-peer connections without central ISPs
  • Local-first AI - On-device inference for content moderation and deliberation
  • Federated services - Email, messaging, file sharing, forums—all self-hosted
  • Ethical deliberation tools - AI-facilitated governance and decision-making
  • Offline resilience - Network functions even without internet connectivity

Why Off-Grid Infrastructure Matters

The Corporate Internet is Capturable

Cloud services, ISPs, social platforms—all controlled by entities that can be pressured, purchased, or shut down. True digital autonomy requires infrastructure sovereignty.

Mesh Networks Enable True Decentralization

When nodes connect directly to each other (not through centralized servers), the network becomes ungovernable—no single point of control or failure.

Local AI Enables Ethical Moderation

Running AI models on local nodes means communities can implement their own ethical standards without relying on corporate content policies.

Technical Architecture

Layer 1: Physical Mesh Network

Hardware Nodes

Raspberry Pi 5 / Orange Pi / Rock 5 with WiFi mesh capabilities

Mesh Protocol

Batman-adv, Yggdrasil, or cjdns for peer-to-peer routing

Network Range

Long-range WiFi (802.11ah) or LoRa for extended reach

Power

Solar-powered nodes for true off-grid operation

Layer 2: Decentralized Services

Communication

Matrix (federated chat), Briar (mesh messaging)

File Sharing

IPFS (content-addressed storage), Syncthing (peer sync)

Forums/Social

ActivityPub-based (Mastodon, Lemmy), or custom local-first forums

Knowledge Base

Local Wikipedia mirrors, shared educational resources

Layer 3: AI-Augmented Deliberation

Local AI Models

Quantized LLMs (Mixtral, Llama) running on each node

Content Moderation

Community-defined ethical guidelines enforced by local AI

Deliberation Tools

AI-facilitated discussions, consensus-building, proposal testing

Philosophical Guardrails

Same immune system from The Whetstone applied to group decisions

The Philosophical Immune System (Applied to Groups)

When communities use The Lyceum for deliberation, proposals are automatically tested against:

  1. Harm Principle - Does this unnecessarily harm anyone?
  2. Rawlsian Veil of Ignorance - Would you support this without knowing your position?
  3. Subsidiarity - Is this being decided at the right level?
  4. Intersectionality - Whose perspectives are missing?
  5. Epistemic Humility - What are we uncertain about?
  6. Consequentialism - Who benefits? Who bears the costs?

Anti-Capture Mechanisms

  • No central servers - Pure peer-to-peer mesh topology
  • Open-source everything - Hardware designs, software, AI models
  • Community governance - Each node/network sets its own rules
  • Local-first AI - No dependence on cloud services or APIs
  • Forkable by design - Any community can take it and adapt it
  • Solar-powered resilience - Functions without grid infrastructure
