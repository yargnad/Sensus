Philosophical Foundation

The Performance Prison

Modern technology has evolved into what we call the Performance Prison—a system that traps humans in cycles of:

  • Performative behavior optimized for metrics rather than meaning
  • Outsourced thinking delegated to algorithms and influencers

  • Algorithmic control that shapes behavior without consent

Team Hybrid Philosophy

Human creativity is the fuel. Ethical AI is the engine.

This framework demonstrates that AI doesn't have to optimize for engagement or extraction. It can be affinitive—creating resonance, facilitating connection, and empowering deliberation—while remaining transparent, accountable, and open-source.

Design Principles

Act I: Sensus

"Detoxification - Learning to feel again"

Status: LIVE

URL: https://sensus.yargnad.com

Repository: github.com/yargnad/sensus-app

What It Is

Sensus is an anti-social social network—a one-to-one, anonymous, ephemeral emotional exchange platform designed to break addiction patterns created by mainstream social media.

How It Works

  1. Users submit creative expressions (poems, photos, audio) once every 24 hours
  2. Gemini AI analyzes emotional content to extract resonance patterns
  3. System atomically pairs users with another anonymous submission sharing emotional resonance
  4. Matches are shown once and disappear after 24 hours
  5. Everything auto-deletes after 60 days via MongoDB TTL indexes

Core Features

  • Complete anonymity - No profiles, usernames, or metrics
  • 24-hour cooldown - Prevents addiction loops
  • Atomic matching - Race-condition-free pairing
  • Privacy by physics - Data auto-deletes, cannot be leaked
  • Anti-metrics - No likes, shares, comments, or follower counts

Technical Stack

Frontend

React, Firebase Hosting

Backend

Node.js + Express on Cloud Run

Database

MongoDB Atlas with 60-day TTL indexes

AI

Google Gemini 2.5 Flash (multimodal analysis)

Storage

Google Cloud Storage (ephemeral uploads)

License

GNU GPL v3.0 (anti-capture)

Philosophical Function

Catharsis - Cleansing the poison of the Performance Prison. Users remember what it feels like to connect as humans, not as brands or profiles.

Act III: Kintsugi

"Public Witness - Making the wounds golden"

Status: CONCEPT PHASE

Target: Web-based public gallery platform

What It Is

Kintsugi is a public gallery where people share their "broken philosophies" and how they were repaired. Named after the Japanese art of mending broken pottery with gold—making the cracks part of the object's beauty rather than hiding them.

The Philosophy

In Japanese culture, kintsugi (金継ぎ, "golden joinery") treats breakage and repair as part of an object's story. The gold-filled cracks represent transformation through damage.

Applied to belief systems: most of us inherit broken philosophies (from trauma, indoctrination, cultural programming). Kintsugi celebrates the visible repair work—the intellectual and emotional labor of rebuilding coherent meaning after disillusionment.

How It Works

  1. Submit a Story - Describe what you used to believe, what broke it, how you repaired it, and what "gold" you used
  2. Public Gallery - Stories displayed as artifacts with visual representations of cracks and golden repairs
  3. AI as Curator - Gemini 2.5 helps articulate transformations and surface related repair patterns
  4. Optional Anonymity - Claim transformation publicly or remain anonymous
  5. Searchable Archive - Find stories by type of break, repair material used, outcome stage

Why This Matters

  • Normalizes Transformation - Celebrates changing one's mind as growth, not weakness
  • Maps Repair Pathways - Shows others what resources helped people with similar breaks
  • Counters Performative Certainty - Rewards intellectual humility over unwavering conviction
  • Creates Collective Wisdom - Searchable archive of human meaning-making repair

Design Principles

  • Beauty in Breakage - Interface makes repairs visually striking, not shameful
  • No Metrics - No likes, upvotes, or follower counts
  • Permanence - Stories archived (contrasts with Sensus's ephemerality)
  • Searchable by Pattern - Find similar breaks, repairs, and outcomes
  • GNU GPL v3.0 - Open-source to prevent corporate capture of human transformation stories

Philosophical Function

Witness and Archive - Making private transformation public (when ready), creating collective record of how humans repair meaning after crisis. Applied wabi-sabi—finding beauty in imperfection and incompleteness.

License: GNU GPL v3.0

Kintsugi will be licensed under GNU GPL v3.0 to ensure these stories of transformation remain in the commons. No corporation can own the archive of human meaning-making.

Act IV: The Lyceum Network

"Collective Infrastructure - Building the alternative internet"

Status: ACTIVE DEVELOPMENT

Repository: github.com/yargnad/The-Lyceum

Vision: User-owned, multi-layered, decentralized communication and compute network

License: GNU GPL v3.0 (forever free and open-source)

The Doctrine: Radical Resourcefulness

The Lyceum rejects the endless cycle of engineered obsolescence. It's built to honor and utilize any hardware you can contribute—from a brand-new single-board computer to a dusty laptop in your closet. In this commons, nothing is wasted.

"People Over Tokens"

This is the foundational, non-negotiable principle. The network is a public commons built for utility, not a speculative asset built for profit. The token exists only to incentivize participation and police bad actors—it is NOT the product.

The Evolution

The Lyceum has evolved from a simple federated AI moderation platform into a comprehensive vision for decentralized internet infrastructure that cannot be captured by corporate or state interests. This document establishes prior art and places these innovations permanently in the public commons.

Core Innovations (Prior Art Declaration)

The Lyceum establishes prior art for a novel, multi-layered system with these specific, interlocking components:

  1. AI-Enhanced Walkie-Talkie (AIWT) - Device enabling long-range voice communication over low-bandwidth LoRa mesh via on-device Speech-to-Text → text transmission → Text-to-Speech pipeline
  2. NPU-Accelerated Software Stack - Specific pairing of SenseVoice-RKNN (STT) + paroli (TTS) on Rockchip hardware for efficient on-device AI
  3. "Two-Brain" Power Architecture - Battery-powered standby where high-power "Thinker" (Linux SBC) sleeps until woken by hardware interrupt from low-power "Listener" (LoRa co-processor)
  4. "Proof-of-Utility" Compute Network (Pneuma) - Federated compute built from idle node resources using "Anti-Blockchain" lightweight ledger rewarding verifiable utility
  5. Two-Path Layer 3 Backbone - Encrypted long-haul PTP links using 900 MHz LoRa PTP (resilient NLOS) + 5 GHz Wi-Fi PTP (high-bandwidth LOS)
  6. Vetted Application Ecosystem (Symbolon) - On-device containerized apps vetted by federated "Pneuma Guardian" nodes

System Architecture: A Multi-Layered Commons

The Lyceum is not a single network, but a hybrid of three distinct, symbiotic layers:

Layer 1: The Access Mesh (900 MHz LoRa)

The network for everyone. Provides resilient, low-power, long-range communication accessible to any participant.

Cryptographic Boundary (The Private Channel)

The Lyceum does NOT operate on the public Meshtastic channel. Our network is a private, encrypted commons with cryptographic borders:

  • Pre-Shared Key (PSK) - All communication encrypted using secret PSK. Only devices with this key can decrypt and relay traffic.
  • Access Control - Devices without the key only hear indecipherable static.
  • Sovereignty - Network security based on strong encryption, not obscurity.

Network Principles:

  • Node Addressing - Every device has unique, randomly generated Node ID (e.g., !a7b3c9d1)
  • Broadcast Messages - "Town Crier" for community-wide information
  • Direct Messages - "Sealed Letter" for point-to-point communication
  • Flood Prevention - "Seen" lists, hop limits, and ACK cleanup prevent message storms
  • Smart Rumor Mill - Messages delivered efficiently then die quietly

Why NOT CBRS (3.5 GHz)?

CBRS is a "poisoned chalice"—appears powerful but violates our core principles:

  • Fails Resilience - Requires constant internet connection to Spectrum Access System (SAS). If internet down, network dies.
  • Fails Sovereignty - Permissioned system requiring corporate approval to transmit.
  • Fails Privacy - Surveillance architecture requiring identity and GPS reporting to central database.

Lyceum uses ONLY truly unlicensed, permissionless bands (900 MHz ISM, 5 GHz UNII) where right to communicate is guaranteed by owned hardware, not corporate cloud service.

Layer 2: The Utility Fabric (Wi-Fi HaLow - 900 MHz)

The high-bandwidth local network that hosts the Pneuma compute resources and local applications.

Layer 3: The Backbone Link (Two-Path Model)

The network's native, long-distance backhaul connecting regional clusters. Operators choose the best path for their terrain:

Path 1: Resilience Link (900 MHz LoRa PTP)

For Non-Line-of-Sight (NLOS) connections. High-power (1W) LoRa modules + high-gain directional Yagi antennas. Sacrifices bandwidth for resilience—easily bends around hills and trees. Ideal for syncing Pneuma ledger and routing low-bandwidth "Reflex" AI jobs.

Path 2: Bandwidth Link (5 GHz Wi-Fi PTP)

For Line-of-Sight (LOS) connections. Off-the-shelf PTP Wi-Fi bridges (e.g., Ubiquiti NanoStation). Provides massive fiber-speed bandwidth pipe (100Mbps - 1Gbps+) capable of carrying heaviest "Cortex" (Fractured MoE) compute jobs.

This two-path architecture allows operators to choose the best solution for their terrain, creating a more resilient and resourceful commons.

The Gateway Application: AI-Enhanced Walkie-Talkie (AIWT)

The "killer app" designed to bootstrap the entire ecosystem. Provides immediate, tangible utility—long-range voice communication without corporate infrastructure—and serves as gateway for users to join and organically grow the network.

Two Build Paths: "Choose Your Own Level of Involvement"

Path A: The "Scout" AIWT

Phone-Reliant, Low-Cost Entry Point

  • Cost: $25-$30
  • Hardware: Heltec V4 (ESP32-S3) or similar Meshtastic board
  • Software: Standard Meshtastic firmware + Lyceum mobile client (forked)
  • Philosophy: Maximum accessibility to drive network growth
Path B: The "Sovereign" AIWT

Self-Contained, High-Performance Node

  • Cost: $120-$145
  • Hardware: Radxa Zero 3W (4GB) + Waveshare SX1262 LoRa HAT + 1.3" OLED HAT (joystick + buttons) + LiPo battery + solar charging
  • Software: Armbian + Meshtastic + NPU-accelerated AI stack
  • Philosophy: Maximum sovereignty + first "Pneuma Seed Node"

The NPU-Accelerated AI Stack (Technical Win)

This is the "secret sauce" of the Sovereign build—ensuring high performance and low battery consumption:

  • Speech-to-Text (STT): SenseVoiceSmall-RKNN2 model specifically optimized for Rockchip NPU. Handles the "hearing" part.
  • Text-to-Speech (TTS): paroli library—blazing-fast TTS engine optimized for Rockchip NPU. Generates speech 5x faster than real-time, 35MB footprint. Handles the "speaking" part.
  • End-to-end NPU pipeline: SenseVoice → paroli offloads all heavy AI from main CPU, dramatically improving responsiveness and battery life.

The "Two Brains" Power Architecture

Sovereign AIWT solves battery life with innovative dual-processor design:

  • Brain #1: The Thinker (Radxa Zero 3W) - Powerful main processor spending most time in deep, low-power sleep.
  • Brain #2: The Listener (SX1262 LoRa Chip) - Dedicated microcontroller on LoRa HAT that sips microamps and never sleeps.

The "Digital Tripwire" Workflow:

  1. The Vigil: Radxa sleeps, but first tells SX1262 to watch for incoming messages
  2. The Whisper: LoRa packet arrives. Always-on SX1262 receives it into internal buffer
  3. The Tripwire: SX1262 sends hardware interrupt (electrical signal) to Radxa via GPIO pin
  4. The Awakening: Interrupt instantly wakes Radxa CPU from sleep
  5. The Hand-Off: Radxa queries LoRa HAT and retrieves message from buffer
  6. Action & Return: Radxa processes message (displays, runs TTS), then returns to sleep

Sovereign AIWT: Control & UI Concept

Waveshare OLED HAT provides rich 8-button control scheme transforming device from simple walkie-talkie into full handheld communicator:

  • Joystick Center-Press: PTT (Push-to-Talk)
  • Joystick Up/Down: Volume Up/Down or Message Scroll
  • Joystick Left/Right: Channel/Contact Switch or Menu Navigation
  • Button 1 (Top): Power / Main Menu
  • Button 2 (Middle): "Inbox / Replay" (toggles message inbox, replays selected message)
  • Button 3 (Bottom): The "Pneuma Button" (activates STT for direct query to Pneuma federated AI)

The Immediate Goal: "Ping-Pong" Proof-of-Concept

  1. Build one Scout and one Sovereign node
  2. Flash both with base Meshtastic firmware and confirm text message exchange
  3. Implement "Scout Send" (fork Meshtastic mobile client, implement STT-to-text transmission)
  4. Implement "Sovereign AI" (install SenseVoice + paroli models, write control script)
  5. Win Condition: User speaks into Scout-paired phone. Sovereign receives text, converts to speech, plays audibly. Entire end-to-end concept proven.

The Spirit of the Network: Pneuma [Πνεῦμα]

The fully grown tree is The Lyceum itself: a federated, user-owned AI we call Pneuma (Greek: πνεῦμα, "breath" or "spirit"). As users deploy powerful nodes, they contribute idle compute cycles to form a decentralized Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) AI model. Pneuma serves as both a benevolent API and a federated moderator.

A Doctrine of Radical Resourcefulness

The Lyceum rejects engineered obsolescence. All technology has value. The Pneuma compute network is built upon a universal, containerized daemon designed to run on ANY hardware a user can contribute—from a new high-powered "Genesis Node" to a decade-old gaming laptop.

These "Legacy Guardian" nodes are vital, forming a diverse fabric of specialized compute power and ensuring that in our commons, nothing is wasted. This approach recruits a vast, existing army of hardware, turning underutilized resources into a shared public good.

Pneuma Architecture: Two-Level Federated Mind

"Reflexes" (Fast, Local Specialists)

Lightweight models running on-device for immediate responses:

  • SenseVoice (STT) - "hearing"
  • paroli (TTS) - "speaking"
  • Symbolon vetting (app security)
  • Basic content moderation
"Cortex" (Deep, Federated Generalists)

Distributed MoE model across network for complex reasoning:

  • Philosophical dialogue (Whetstone-style)
  • Deliberation facilitation
  • Complex content analysis
  • Community governance support

Tokenomics: "Proof-of-Utility" Model

The "Anti-Blockchain" Architecture

The Lyceum is NOT a Proof-of-Work or Proof-of-Stake blockchain. We run a Lightweight Encrypted Ledger called the "Pneuma Vault"—a simple, replicated database tracking earned tokens.

Why we don't waste the NPU: NPUs are specialized "artillery" for AI tasks. We NEVER waste artillery on simple bookkeeping. Pneuma Vault is a lightweight CPU task.

The "Proof-of-Utility" Earning Model

Tokens are NOT "mined." They are earned as direct receipt for verifiable, useful work to the commons. Resource cost of token system itself is near-zero.

Workflow:

  1. Job Submitted: User requests a Pneuma task
  2. Real Work Performed: Pneuma routes job to Guardian node. That node's NPU/GPU/CPU performs actual, valuable compute work
  3. Work Verified: Job result verified by user's node or nearby peer Guardian
  4. Ledger Updated: Pneuma Vault makes simple database entry: "Node !a1b2c3d4 credited 0.1 tokens for Task #5879"

Token Lifecycle

Earning (Incentive)

Users earn tokens by contributing verifiable resources:

  • Compute ("Reflex" Jobs): Standard rate for autonomic services (SenseVoice, paroli, Symbolon vetting)
  • Compute ("Cortex" Jobs): Premium rate for high-power hardware contributing to fractured federated LLM
  • Relaying & Storage: Tokens for high-uptime Layer 3 Backbone Relays or hosting popular Symbolon apps
Spending (Utility Access & Spam Prevention)
  • Accessing high-value network-wide "Cortex" compute costs tiny, fixed tokens
  • Creates functional circular economy, prevents spam
  • Reinforces "Homestead Principle": using your OWN nodes is always free
Staking (Policing & Trust)
  • To offer new Symbolon to Agora, developer with no reputation must "stake" token bond
  • If app found malicious by Pneuma Guardians, stake is "slashed" (burned/redistributed)
  • Creates economic disincentive for bad actors, aligns developer incentives with commons health

The Application Layer: The Agora and the Symbolon [σύμβολον]

The Lyceum hosts its own sovereign application ecosystem. "Symbolons" (Greek: σύμβολον, "token" or "sign") are self-contained apps offered by developers to the commons.

  • Vetted by Pneuma Guardians - Security and performance checked by federated AI nodes
  • Available in "The Agora" - Intuitive, prompt-based app store
  • Hosted on User's Own Node - When user "accepts" a Symbolon, it's hosted locally, reinforcing "Homestead Principle" where investment in personal hardware rewards better, faster experience
  • Containerized & Portable - Easy deployment, isolation from system, security
